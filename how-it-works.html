How it works

Four steps, from official source to clean feed.

We prefer official feeds and sitemaps over aggressive scraping, so the data stays legitimate, low-risk and reliable — and higher quality, because it comes from the source's own structured output.

01

Discover

We build and maintain a master registry of official sources — starting from curated lists of roughly 2,500 government and public-interest sites, cleaned, deduplicated and normalized into one reliable list with a stable schema: source type, jurisdiction, homepage, press room, feed and sitemap URLs.

02

Monitor

Where a structured feed exists, we use it. We prefer official RSS, XML and Atom feeds, sitemaps and press-room indexes — collecting on polite, capped schedules that respect how publishers publish. High-value sources are captured across every stream, not pointed at a single news page.

03

Structure

Every document is normalized into one consistent NewsML-style schema and exported as clean XML or CSV — sourced, timestamped, with clean dates and body text. Indexes, navigation and boilerplate are filtered out. The relevance review is the product, not overhead.

04

Review & deliver

We work in small batches with logs, checkpoints and manual review, keeping raw, working and final data separate. Before any client delivery, a hard QA gate blocks five defect classes — non-articles, boilerplate, duplicates, encoding errors and wrong dates — so nothing ships unchecked.

Why feeds over scraping

Legitimate by design, not by exception.

Aggressive HTML scraping breaks constantly and strains publishers. By prioritizing official feeds, sitemaps and public indexes, we stay legitimate, low-risk and reliable — and data quality is higher because it comes from the source's own structured output.
